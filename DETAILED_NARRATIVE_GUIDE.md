# Detailed Narrative Writing Guide for Security Reports

## Overview

This guide ensures ALL security incidents are written in the detailed narrative format matching the official Security Situation Report standards.

---

The suspects are scheduled to be produced before the Magistrate court, Embilipitiya on the 18th March 2026. (OTM.1421)-A

---

## Required Elements (Every Incident MUST Have)

### 1. Station Name
- **Format:** UPPERCASE followed by colon
- **Example:** `EMBILIPITIYA:`

### 2. Incident Title (Summary)
- **Format:** In (parentheses), brief description
- **Example:** `(Arrest of suspects along with a detonator and gunpowder)`

### 3. Date and Time
- **Format:** "On the [ordinal] [Month] [Year]"
- **Example:** `On the 17th March 2026`
- **Always use:** 17th, 18th, 21st, 22nd, 23rd, 31st (ordinal suffixes)

### 4. Context/Source
- **Format:** How information was received
- **Examples:**
  - `acting on an information received through the 1-1-9 project`
  - `based on a tip-off from the public`
  - `during a routine patrol`
  - `following intelligence reports`

### 5. Action Taken
- **Format:** What police did
- **Examples:**
  - `police arrested`
  - `police recovered`
  - `police seized`
  - `officers conducted a search`

### 6. Person Details (COMPLETE)
For EACH person mentioned, include:
- **Title:** Rev., Mr., Mrs., Dr., etc.
- **Full Name:** First and last name
- **Age:** "aged XX"
- **Position/Occupation:** (if relevant) "the chief incumbent of..."
- **Complete Address:** House/plot number, street, area, district
  - **Example:** `aged 56 of # 896, Mayuragama, Sewanagala`

### 7. Item Details (SPECIFIC)
For EACH item, include:
- **Exact Quantity:** Numbers, weights, counts
  - ✅ `80g of gunpowder`
  - ✅ `2 electric detonators`
  - ✅ `15 rounds of 9mm ammunition`
  - ❌ `some gunpowder`
  - ❌ `several detonators`
- **Specific Description:**
  - ✅ `electric detonator`
  - ✅ `T-56 assault rifle`
  - ✅ `locally made muzzle loading firearm`
  - ❌ `weapon`
  - ❌ `explosive device`

### 8. Circumstances/Context
- **What was happening:** `while digging a tunnel`
- **Purpose/Intent:** `with the intention of treasure hunting`
- **Location specifics:** `in the temple premises`

### 9. Current Status/Next Steps
- **Court proceedings:** `scheduled to be produced before the Magistrate court, Embilipitiya on the 18th March 2026`
- **Investigation status:** `Investigations are being conducted`
- **Custody:** `remanded until [date]`

### 10. Reference Code
- **Format:** (CODE.NUMBER)-LETTER
- **Examples:**
  - `(OTM.1421)-A`
  - `(CTM.530)-M`
  - `(IR.2456)-E`

## Writing Templates by Incident Type

### ARREST WITH ITEMS RECOVERED

```
[STATION]: ([Brief summary]) On the [date], [context/source], police arrested [Title] [Full Name], aged [XX], [occupation/position], [complete address] [and additional persons with same details] along with [specific items with quantities], [circumstances]. The suspect(s) [is/are] scheduled to be produced before the [Court Name] on the [date]. ([REFERENCE])-[X]
```

### RECOVERY WITHOUT ARREST

```
[STATION]: ([Brief summary]) On the [date], [context/source], police recovered [specific items with quantities] from [specific location]. [Additional details about discovery]. [Ownership/investigation status]. ([REFERENCE])-[X]
```

### ARREST FOR ACTIVITY

```
[STATION]: ([Brief summary]) On the [date], [context/source], police arrested [complete person details] for [specific activity/charge]. [Circumstances and evidence]. [Legal action taken]. ([REFERENCE])-[X]
```

## Professional Language Standards

### ✅ USE (Formal/Professional)
- `police arrested`
- `acting on information received`
- `scheduled to be produced before`
- `investigations are being conducted`
- `in possession of`
- `along with`
- `aged [number]`
- `of # [house number]`
- `the following persons`
- `with the intention of`

### ❌ AVOID (Informal/Vague)
- `cops caught`
- `got a tip`
- `will go to court`
- `looking into it`
- `had`
- `with`
- `[number] years old`
- `from [area]` (without house number)
- `some people`
- `trying to`

## Quantity and Measurement Standards

### Weights
- ✅ `80g of gunpowder`
- ✅ `2.5kg of explosives`
- ✅ `150mg of heroin`

### Counts

- ✅ `15 rounds of ammunition`
- ✅ `3 hand grenades`

### Weapons
- ✅ `T-56 assault rifle with serial number XYZ123`
- ✅ `locally made pistol`
- ✅ `9mm semi-automatic pistol`

### Explosives
- ✅ `electric detonator`
- ✅ `non-electric detonator`
- ✅ `C-4 plastic explosive`

## Address Format Standards

### Complete Address Format
```
[Title] [Name], aged [XX] of # [house/plot number], [street/area], [locality], [district]
```

### Examples
- ✅ `aged 56 of # 896, Mayuragama, Sewanagala`
- ✅ `aged 27 of #14/2 Panahaduwa, Kolombage-Ara`
- ✅ `of Pallimune-East, Mannar`
- ❌ `from Colombo`
- ❌ `living in Galle area`

---

## Date and Time Format

### Date Format
- ✅ `On the 17th March 2026`
- ✅ `on the 18th of March 2026`
- ❌ `On March 17, 2026`
- ❌ `On 17/03/2026`

### Time Format (when specified)
- ✅ `at 0400 hrs.`
- ✅ `at approximately 1530 hrs.`
- ✅ `between 2000 hrs. and 2200 hrs.`

---

## Court and Legal References

### Court Proceedings
- ✅ `scheduled to be produced before the Magistrate court, [Location] on the [date]`
- ✅ `remanded until [date]`
- ✅ `released on bail`

### Investigation Status
- ✅ `Investigations are being conducted`
- ✅ `Further investigations are underway`
- ✅ `The matter is under investigation`


## Summary

Every security incident MUST be written as a complete, detailed narrative paragraph with:
1. All names, ages, and addresses
2. Specific quantities and descriptions
3. Full context and circumstances
4. Current status and next steps
5. Professional formal language
6. 100-300 words per incident

This ensures 100% consistency with official Security Situation Report standards.
