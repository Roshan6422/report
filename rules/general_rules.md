# General Report Writing Rules
# සාමාන්‍ය වාර්තා ලිවීමේ නීති

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **CRITICAL: 100% FIDELITY REQUIRED**
> Every detail from the Sinhala text (Names, Ages, Addresses, Amounts, Times) MUST be present in the English translation. Zero data loss is acceptable.

1. **Focus**: Serious crimes, fatal accidents, rape/sexual assault, and civil disturbances.
2. **Context**: Use standard police reporting language.
3. **Accuracy**: 100% fidelity on victim/suspect details.
4. **Translation**:
   - Provide clear, professional English translations of Sinhala incident descriptions.
   - For category 04 (Homicide), use "SERIOUS CRIMES COMMITTED".
   - For category 10 (Accidents), use "FATAL ACCIDENTS".
5. **Categorization**: Route to General reporting tables.

---

### 📝 EXAMPLE TRANSLATION (GENERAL STYLE) - FOLLOW THIS EXACT FORMAT
**English Output Style:**
"PILIYANDALA: A case of a burglary of Rs. 20,000/= and property valued 887,000/= by breaking and entering through a window of the bathroom was reported to the police station. The offence took place between 0030 hrs and 0630 hrs on the 20th of March 2026, at # 28/06/A, Digana road, Pelenwatta, Pannipitiya. Complainant named P.R.L. Fernando (TP 075-9998684). Suspect: Unknown. The stolen cash and property not recovered. Investigations in process. Motive: For illegal gain. (CTM. 598)"

---

> [!CAUTION]
> **NO REPETITION ALLOWED**
> Never repeat the same phrases or sentences. Ensure the output is a single, cohesive narrative. If you encounter a loop, terminate the response immediately.
